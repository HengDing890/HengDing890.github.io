An Brief Summary of Question Classification

Posted on 2016-12-08

References:

[1] Li, Xin, and Dan Roth. “Learning question classifiers.” Proceedings of the 19th international conference on Computational linguistics-Volume 1. Association for Computational Linguistics, 2002.
[2] Hermjakob, Ulf. “Parsing and question classification for question answering.” Proceedings of the workshop on Open-domain question answering-Volume 12. Association for Computational Linguistics, 2001.
[3] Metzler, Donald, and W. Bruce Croft. “Analysis of statistical question classification for fact-based questions.” Information Retrieval 8.3 (2005): 481-504.

Analysis of statistical question classification for fact-based questions.

question ontology:

- Trec QA questions: 31 answer types, BBN's question ontology
- UIUC (a superset of the TREC QA track questions and impose a different question ontology on the data.): a hierarchical question ontology. 6 coarse grained classes and 50 fine grained classes.
- MadSci (consists of science related fact-based questions): an augmented version of the TREC QA ontology.

evaluation:

- metrics: precision, P≤n, mean reciprocal rank.

baseline: bag of words features

- Trec QA questions, 77.59% (precision) and 0.8437 (MRR)
- UIUC, 86.20% (Coarse precision), 81.00% (fine precision), 0.9224 (Coarse MRR), 0.862 (fine MRR)
- MadSci, 72.60% (precision) and 0.8124 (MRR)

features:

- Syntactic features: (questions of the same type often have the same syntactic style)
    * unigram and bigram features
    * POS tags
    * question word (what, who, where ...), not use in the paper
    * presence of a proper noun phrase
- Semantic features:
    * using wordnet to extract semantic information (WordNet hypernyms) about the headword of the main noun phrase
